# Beginner's Cybersecurity Checklist

Staying safe online doesn't have to be complicated. By following these ten simple best practices, you can significantly protect your personal data and devices from common cyber threats.

---

## 1. Use Strong and Unique Passwords
* Avoid using easily guessable passwords like "123456" or reusing the same password across multiple websites. 
* Instead, create long passwords that combine letters, numbers, and special characters to make them hard to crack. 
* Using a password manager can help you safely store and automatically generate these complex passwords.

## 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
* Two-Factor Authentication adds an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring two forms of identification before logging in. 
* Even if someone steals your password, they won't be able to access your account without the second verification step, which is usually a code sent to your phone. 
* Always turn this feature on for your email, banking, and social media accounts.

## 3. Keep Software Up to Date
* Software updates frequently patch critical security vulnerabilities that hackers could exploit to gain control of your system. 
* Make sure to regularly update your operating system, web browsers, and installed applications. 
* Whenever possible, turn on automatic updates so your devices remain protected without manual effort.

## 4. Avoid Clicking Suspicious Links
* Cybercriminals often use phishing tactics, sending deceptive links via email or text messages that mimic legitimate companies to steal your credentials. 
* Always double-check the sender's details and think twice before clicking on links you weren't expecting. 
* If a link looks strange, navigate directly to the official website using your browser instead of clicking it.

## 5. Download Software Only from Trusted Sources
* Downloading files or programs from third-party sites greatly increases the risk of accidentally installing malicious software. 
* Only download programs from official websites, authorized app stores, or verified repositories. 
* Be cautious of cracked software or free downloads that normally require a paid license.

## 6. Back Up Important Data
* Hardware failures, physical loss, or ransomware attacks can cause you to lose your precious files permanently. 
* Regularly back up your important documents, photos, and projects to an external hard drive or a secure cloud service. 
* Having a reliable backup ensures you can quickly recover your files if the worst happens.

## 7. Lock Your Devices
* Physical security is just as important as digital security when it comes to protecting your personal space. 
* Always lock your smartphone, laptop, or tablet with a strong PIN, password, or biometric lock whenever you step away from it. 
* This simple habit prevents unauthorized people from browsing through your sensitive data if your device is left unattended.

## 8. Use Secure Wi-Fi Networks
* Public Wi-Fi networks in coffee shops, airports, and hotels are often unencrypted and highly vulnerable to network snooping. 
* Avoid conducting sensitive business, like online banking or typing credentials, while connected to these open networks. 
* If you must use public Wi-Fi, protect your data traffic by using a trustworthy Virtual Private Network (VPN).

## 9. Be Careful with Email Attachments
* Email attachments are a classic delivery system used by attackers to spread computer viruses and spyware. 
* Never open attachments from unknown senders or unexpected files sent out of the blue by people you do know. 
* If a friend sends you an unusual file, verify with them through a text or call before opening it.

## 10. Keep Antivirus Software Updated
* Antivirus software acts as your computer's primary defense system by scanning for and neutralizing digital threats. 
* For it to work effectively against the newest viruses, its threat database needs to be constantly updated. 
* Set your antivirus software to run regular background scans and update its definitions automatically.
